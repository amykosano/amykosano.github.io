Manifesto

I combine my passion for immortalizing stories and theater production by adapting them to the screen for more people to see. Not only do I work on costumes for Macbeth and Little Shop of Horrors, I also take photographs of my peers hard at work in carpentry and theater technology to help promote the live performances. Even now, as a student nearing my early graduation, I love learning new skills and meeting new people.

I love using what I know to help others. Before I interned as a wardrobe assistant for a Lifetime film, I volunteered as one of the two main developers for an hour-long audio tour soundtrack for an exhibit at the Storm King Art Center in NY. While I enjoy working as part of a team, both in-person and remotely, I am self-sufficient and able to work independently. For example, I worked remotely on my previously mentioned audio tour for 11 months and had meetings every Monday morning.
